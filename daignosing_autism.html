Decreased attention to what parents say may help diagnose autism.

A new study suggests that how attentive young children are to their mother's words may be a useful indicator for early diagnosis of autism.

Scientists at the Diego School of Medicine found that toddlers with spectrum disorders have found that they pay less attention to the speech forms that adults use when speaking to young children.

In addition, toddlers who paid less attention to exaggerated speech had lower social and verbal skills.

The researchers analyzed the reactions of 653 toddlers with and without autism.

The team had the toddlers watch two videos. In one, a woman was shown talking in the way mothers often use when speaking to their babies.

On the other, the researchers showed the toddlers abstract shapes.

The researchers found that toddlers without ASD paid attention to the woman’s speech around 82% of the time.

Autistic toddlers responded to infant-directed speech with varying degrees of attention, ranging from 0%-100%, demonstrating a broad spectrum of engagement.
